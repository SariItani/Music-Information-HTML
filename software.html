    Some software used to work with music and sound editting:

  • Garageband

    Garageband runs on macOS only, and it's completely free; providing a wide varity of options and a friendly GUI like no other DAW (Digital Audio Workstation), it is the most volatile and easy to use music production software out there.

  • Audacity

    Audacity runs on any device, and it's completely free. It allows you to view and edit sound waves and analyze frequencies and provides you with much more sound engineering tools, and since it's lightweight and user-friendly, it is the best sound engineering software available.

  • LMMS

    “Made by musicians, for musicians!” If you are a professional music producer, LMMS runs on all known operating systems, and is lightweight and has an easy to learn GUI with lots of pro features that makes the producers' lives easier.

  • FLStudio

    FLStudio is available on windows and macOS, but is a paid application. However, it is used by most music producers in the music industry on a professional level because of its spectacular capabilities.

  • Bandlab

    This free workstation available for windows, macOS, and android devices, is one of the best software to ever exist for producing music. It has many features similar to Garageband, so it is considered to be the windows-android counter part of Garageband.
